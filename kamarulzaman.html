A Proud Son of Perlis

Person-In-Charge · Kampung Wai River Tourism

Kamarulzaman bin Ahmad, 36, is a dedicated local from Perlis who serves as the Person-in-Charge (PIC) at Kampung Wai — one of Perlis's most charming riverside ecotourism destinations. Married with three children, he brings a deep sense of community pride to his work every single day.

Having worked at Kampung Wai for over a year, Kamarulzaman coordinates a full team of 16 workers who manage boat trips, kayak packages, and beca rides for both local and international visitors. His leadership ensures smooth operations whether on a bustling weekend or a quiet weekday.

During school holidays, Kampung Wai sees up to 15 trips per boat in a single day — a testament to the growing popularity of this unique Perlis experience. Kamarulzaman is the steady hand that keeps everything running safely and warmly.

"Kampung Wai is our home, and every visitor that comes is our guest. We want people to feel the beauty of Perlis the way we do — through the water, the hills, and the fresh air."

Remarkable Facts About Kampung Wai

🏔️

500-Million-Year-Old Hills

The stunning limestone hills flanking both sides of the Kampung Wai canal date back an astonishing 500 million years, making the scenery truly prehistoric and unique in Malaysia.

🌊

22km Flood Diversion Canal

The canal stretches 22km from Timah Tasoh to Kuala Perlis. It was originally built as a flood diversion project for Tasik Timah Tasoh, channelling excess water safely to the sea.

🕳️

Longest Water Tunnel in Malaysia

The Terowong Lencongan Banjir Barat Timah Tasoh cuts through Bukit Tebing Tinggi for 765 metres, making it the longest water tunnel in Malaysia and a highlight of every kayak trip.

About the Destination

A Hidden Gem of Perlis

Kampung Wai is nestled within vibrant flora and fauna in Kuala Perlis, known for its serene environment, fresh air, and breathtaking natural views. It has grown to become one of the top attractions during the Visit Perlis Year 2024–2026 campaign.

Visitors can enjoy feeding fish by the riverbank, kayaking through the crystal-clear water, going on a river boat ride, or simply relaxing in the calming natural surroundings. Evening visits are especially popular for witnessing stunning sunset views over the ancient limestone hills.

Local food and drink stalls are also available at the Dataran Kampung Wai, making it a complete recreational destination for families and adventure seekers alike.

How to Visit

Getting to Kampung Wai

🚆

By Train + Bus

Take the ETS to Arau Royal Railway Station, then walk to the nearby bus stop and take bus T10 to Kangar. From Kangar, board bus T12 which goes directly to Kampung Wai.

🚌

From Kuala Lumpur

Take a direct bus to Kuala Perlis Terminal, then board bus T12 which heads straight to Kampung Wai. A convenient and affordable option from the capital.

🚗

By Personal Transport

The most convenient option. Parking is available on the right side before the bridge, with additional overflow parking at a nearby resident's compound on the left.

His Work & Impact

Role & Contribution

How Kamarulzaman contributes to tourism, the community, and the heritage of Perlis through his dedicated work at Kampung Wai.

01

Driving Perlis River Tourism

As PIC, Kamarulzaman is the backbone of river tourism operations at Kampung Wai. He coordinates all boat, kayak and beca activities — ensuring smooth daily operations, particularly during peak weekends when over 70 trips take place. His work directly supports the Visit Perlis Year 2024–2026 campaign.

02

Creating Jobs for the Local Community

Kamarul's operations support 16 dedicated boat workers and 5–6 beca operators — all from the local Perlis community. By providing steady income for these individuals and their families, he plays a vital role in the economic wellbeing of Kampung Wai residents.

03

Welcoming Tourists from All Over Malaysia

Kampung Wai attracts visitors from states like Perak, Kedah, Selangor and beyond. Kamarulzaman and his team ensure that every tourist — whether first-timers or returning visitors — leaves with a safe and memorable experience, building Perlis's reputation as a premier eco-tourism destination.

04

School Holiday Tourism Management

During school holidays, demand surges dramatically. Kamarul scales up operations to handle up to 15 trips per boat, ensuring families are well-served and wait times are managed efficiently. His planning keeps Kampung Wai running smoothly even during peak crowd periods.

05

Preserving Malaysian Heritage Through Beca

By integrating beca (trishaw) rides into the Kampung Wai tourism experience, Kamarul helps keep this traditional form of Malaysian transport alive. What could have faded into history is instead given new life as a cultural tourism attraction, especially meaningful for younger visitors experiencing it for the first time.

🌿 A Local Hero of Perlis Tourism

Kamarulzaman bin Ahmad may be just over a year into his role, but his dedication, local knowledge, and genuine passion for Perlis have already made a lasting impact on Kampung Wai. He is not just managing tourism — he is building a legacy for his community, one river trip at a time.
